# TOI-5205b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

TOI-5205b is a warm sub-Neptune (Teq ≈ 850 K) with mass 0.79 M_J and radius 1.4 R_J orbiting an F-star every 3.66 days. Transitioning between hot Jupiters and sub-Neptunes, TOI-5205b exhibits balanced H/He envelope with significant molecular content.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 92-95%
- **Water vapor**: ~2-4%
- **Methane**: ~0.8-1.5%
- **Ammonia**: ~0.3-0.8%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.2-0.4%

### Bulk Density: 0.28 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 25-30 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |

- **Water vapor**: 150-300 ppm

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~4-6σ

## Summary

TOI-5205b bridges between hot Jupiters and sub-Neptunes with warm dayside temperature (850 K). Strong water and methane features detectable with JWST observations. Balanced molecular chemistry ideal for studying mid-range exoplanet atmospheres.
