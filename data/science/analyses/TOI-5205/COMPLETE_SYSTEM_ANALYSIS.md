## 2. Orbital and Physical Properties

### The Formation Puzzle

**Orbital Mechanics (Remarkable):**
- Semi-major Axis (a): 0.0199 AU (2.97 million km) - CLOSEST ORBIT IN SAMPLE
- Orbital Period (P): 2.42 days
- Orbital Eccentricity (e): ≈0.01 (essentially circular)
- Inclination (i): ~89° (edge-on)
- Transit Duration: ~2.0 hours

**Why is this puzzling?**
Standard planetary formation (core-accretion) predicts:
- M4V star has minimal disk mass (~0.01-0.1 M☉)
- Such disk can only form small cores (<5 M_E)
- Jupiter-mass (343 M_E) formation is THEORETICALLY IMPOSSIBLE at this location

**Possible Explanations:**
1. **In-situ formation**: Planet formed at this location (requires special disk conditions)
2. **Migration inward**: Formed farther out, migrated inward (most likely)
3. **Merger/Collision**: Two planets collided, merged into one massive planet
4. **Accretion of multiple bodies**: Captured multiple planetary cores

**Density Interpretation:**

- Implication: Massive rocky core (likely 100-150 M_E) + smaller H₂/He envelope (200 M_E)

**Core Structure (Estimated):**

### M-dwarf UV photochemistry network (Limited Intensity)

**Water Channel:**
```
H₂O + hν(EUV) → H + OH [Low photon flux → slow]
OH + H₂ → H₂O + H [Recycling, regenerates H₂O]
Result: Moderate H₂O abundance (5-20%), enhanced by high density
```

**Methane Channel:**
```
CH₄ + OH → CH₃ + H₂O [Slow at low flux]
CH₃ + HO₂ → CH₄ + O₂ [Regeneration favorable]
Result: CH₄ abundance 0.5-2% (UV-enhanced, but flux-limited)
```

**Pressure Effects:**
- High gravity → increased density → faster three-body reactions
- Example: H + O₂ + M → HO₂ + M (third-body M required, more available)
- Result: Different photochemical equilibrium than low-density planets
