================================================================================
Planet Name:             WASP-69b
System Type:             Neptune-like warm Jupiter around K-type dwarf
Temperature Profile:     Warmest cool exoplanet regime (∼1000 K)
Discovery Status:        Known system, limited characterization

================================================================================

Semi-major Axis:         0.0453 AU

Carbon Monoxide:         Present from photochemistry
Methane:                 Possible and even likely at this T

COMPARATIVE CONTEXT
================================================================================
Similar Systems:        HAT-P-18b (cooler hot Jupiter), warm Neptunes
K-dwarf host:      Very strong (low UV dramatically changes chemistry)
Key Distinction:        Coolest analyzed; richest molecular chemistry expected
Temperature Bridge:     Between hot Jupiters and cool Neptunes

SCIENTIFIC PRIORITIES
================================================================================
1. Detect water, CO, CO₂, methane simultaneously (complete molecular inventory)
2. Characterize chemical photochemistry at low UV environment
3. Measure scale height (large = excellent spectroscopy!)
4. Determine methane presence/absence (novel for this regime)
5. Constrain atmospheric circulation (efficient heat transport?)
6. Measure albedo and heat redistribution efficiency
7. Compare with hot Jupiters on K-dwarfs (WASP-52b difference)
8. Test atmospheric escape theory at very low T

CHALLENGES & OPPORTUNITIES
================================================================================
Challenges:
  - Very faint host star (K-dwarf) reduces transmission depth by factor
  - Multiple overlapping molecular features (spectral deconvolution complex)
  - Long orbital period (~3.86 days) limits observation frequency
  - Internal heat contribution may complicate thermal analysis

Opportunities:
  - GIANT scale height (4-4.5 km!) allows exceptional spectroscopic sensitivity
  - Multiple molecular detections expected (first true molecular atmosphere study)
  - Methane detection would be transformative (unprecedented for hot planets)
  - Low atmospheric escape allows pristine chemical characterization
  - Ideal test case for chemical equilibrium models at low UV

KEY PHYSICAL PROCESSES
================================================================================
Dominant Mechanism:      Photochemical equilibrium (not dissociation)
Escape Process:          Negligible hydrogen loss
Energy Source:           Stellar heating + internal planet heat
Timescale:              Atmosphere remains stable >10 Gyr
Final Fate:             Stable warm Neptune analog

NOTES & CAVEATS
================================================================================
- Lowest temperature of 7-planet sample; richest molecular chemistry
- K-dwarf produces ~50% of UV from solar analogs (major chemistry driver)
- Very low gravity (relative to mass) produces large scale height
- Scale height estimate uses mean molecular weight ~2.8 amu (H₂O-dominated)
- Atmospheric composition assumes solar baseline plus methane consideration
- Hydrogen escape rate negligible; atmosphere is primordial
- This system is IDEAL for JWST's molecular spectroscopy capabilities
- May represent "local" analog to many exoplanets around M-dwarfs
