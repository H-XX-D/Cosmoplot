## Summary

WASP-69b represents the sub-Neptune regime with prominent H/He envelope and massive core. Mild dayside temperature (800 K) with small day-night temperature difference (~100-200 K) creates stable, well-mixed chemistry. Water, methane, and ammonia all detectable at high SNR, making this world ideal for studying mid-sized planet atmospheres. Extended water/ammonia cloud layers dominate transmission spectrum.
