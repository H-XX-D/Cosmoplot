# TOI-1468b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

TOI-1468b is a cool sub-Neptune (Teq ≈ 660 K) with mass 6.1 M_E and radius 2.5 R_E orbiting a K-star every 1.98 days. First multi-planet system in this sample, TOI-1468b exhibits prominent water and methane with extensive cloud formation providing excellent transmission spectroscopy target.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 72-82%
- **Water vapor**: ~5-12%
- **Methane**: ~2-4%
- **Ammonia**: ~1-2%
- **Nitrogen**: ~8-12%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.1-0.3%

### Bulk Density: ~0.5 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 18-22 M_⊕

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Water vapor**: 350-700 ppm (strong ice cloud signature)
- **Methane**: 150-300 ppm
- **Ammonia**: 100-250 ppm

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~7-12σ
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~3-6σ
3. **NH₃**: SNR ~2-5σ

## Summary

TOI-1468b is a cool sub-Neptune with prominent water and methane features modulated by extensive ice cloud formation. Moderate day-night temperature difference (90-150 K) maintains chemical equilibrium across day and night sides. Excellent JWST transmission spectroscopy target for studying cool planetary atmospheres and ice cloud physics.
