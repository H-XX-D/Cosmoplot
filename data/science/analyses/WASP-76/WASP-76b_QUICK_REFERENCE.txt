CHALLENGES & OPPORTUNITIES
================================================================================
Challenges:
  - Extremely complex atmospheric photochemistry
  - TiO/VO particles may dominate opacity (hard to see molecular features)
  - High wind speeds complicate spectral interpretation
  - Strong day/night asymmetry difficult to model

Opportunities:
  - Ideal test case for F-dwarf exoplanet chemistry
  - High-precision TiO/VO characterization possible
  - Strong transmission features enable sensitive observations
  - Extreme regime pushes atmospheric physics limits

NOTES & CAVEATS
================================================================================
- F7 host produces intense UV (factor 2-3 more than solar analogs)
- Extreme radius inflation (1.847 R_J) unusual; possibly young system
- TiO/VO opacity is observationally confirmed (unlike estimates for WASP-19b)
- Temperature profile assumes radiative equilibrium; 3D modeling important
- Hydrogen escape rate scaled from similar ultra-hot systems
- Young age may affect stellar wind properties
- Future observations will refine all parameters
