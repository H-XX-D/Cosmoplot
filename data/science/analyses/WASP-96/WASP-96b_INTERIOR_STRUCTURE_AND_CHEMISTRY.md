# WASP-96b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-96b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1449 K) with mass 0.52 M_J and radius 1.43 R_J orbiting a G-type star every 3.41 days. Low-mass inflated hot Jupiter (density 0.21 g/cm³) with pronounced water vapor absorption making it an ideal JWST transmission spectroscopy target.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2% (strong absorption)
- **Methane**: ~0.2-0.4%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic H**: 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴

### Bulk Density: 0.21 g/cm³ (extremely inflated)

### Core Mass: 10-12 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 1449 |
| 0.8 | 8 | 1270 |
| 0.6 | 80 | 1080 |
| 0.4 | 800 | 900 |
| 0.2 | 8,000 | 750 |

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 80-180 ppm (extended, low-mass atmosphere)
- **Water vapor**: 200-400 ppm (prominent, dominated feature)
- **Methane**: 60-140 ppm
- **CO**: 30-80 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1 μm | 80-150 | Continuum |
| 3.2 μm | 140-250 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 100-180 | CO + H₂O |
| 8 μm | 110-200 | CH₄ |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~5-8σ (strongest feature)
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~2-4σ
3. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ
4. **NH₃**: SNR ~1-2σ

---

## Summary

WASP-96b is one of the lowest-density hot Jupiters known, making it an exceptional transmission spectroscopy target. Extremely extended atmosphere (H/He scale height ~200-300 km) creates enormous molecular features. Water vapor dominates transmission spectrum with ~200-400 ppm depth, making WASP-96b one of the best targets for JWST observations of hot Jupiter atmospheres. This world exemplifies how low mass and high irradiation create extended atmospheres ideal for atmospheric characterization.
