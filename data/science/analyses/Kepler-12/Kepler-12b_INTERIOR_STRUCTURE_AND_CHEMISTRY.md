# Kepler-12b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

Kepler-12b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1479 K) with mass 1.41 M_J and radius 1.79 R_J orbiting a G-type star every 4.44 days. Extremely inflated (density 0.24 g/cm³), Kepler-12b demonstrates the importance of extended atmospheres in hot Jupiters and shows strong molecular absorption in transmission.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Methane**: ~0.2-0.4%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Ammonia**: Trace
- **Atomic H**: 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴

### Bulk Density: 0.24 g/cm³ (extremely inflated)

### Core Mass: 15-20 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 1479 |
| 0.8 | 8 | 1280 |
| 0.6 | 80 | 1080 |
| 0.4 | 800 | 900 |

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (1479 K)
- **3.2 μm**: H₂O strong feature
- **4.7 μm**: CO (40-100 ppm)
- **8 μm**: CH₄ (50-100 ppm)

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 60-150 ppm (extended atmosphere)
- **Water vapor**: 150-350 ppm (strong)
- **Methane**: 50-120 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) |
|-----------|------------|
| 1 μm | 80-150 |
| 3.2 μm | 120-200 |
| 4.7 μm | 70-130 |
| 8 μm | 80-150 |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~4-6σ
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~2-4σ
3. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ
4. **NH₃**: SNR ~1-2σ

## Summary

Kepler-12b is an extremely inflated hot Jupiter with one of the lowest densities among the sample. Extended atmosphere and moderate dayside temperature (1479 K) create strong molecular absorption features across multiple wavelengths. Water vapor dominates transmission spectrum, while methane and CO are also detectable. This world exemplifies how extended atmospheres enhance transmission spectroscopy capabilities for JWST observations.
