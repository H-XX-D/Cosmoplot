CHALLENGES & OPPORTUNITIES
================================================================================
Challenges:
  - Low mass creates unusual radius inflation (interior structure uncertain)
  - Multiple overlapping molecular features (spectral complexity)
  - Faint Kepler-mission star may reduce SNR relative to WASP systems
  - Large scale height means extended atmosphere; transit timing effects possible

Opportunities:
  - GIANT scale height (4.5-5.0 km) yields unprecedented sensitivity
  - Multiple molecular detections expected (rich spectroscopy)
  - Test models of gas giant inflation at extreme limit
  - First opportunity to study H₂-to-gravity ratio effects
  - Large transit depth (largest or second-largest in sample) enables precision spectroscopy

NOTES & CAVEATS
================================================================================
- Extremely low density (0.11 g/cm³) makes this most inflated planet in sample
- G-dwarf host produces moderate UV (less than F-dwarfs, more than K-dwarfs)
- Lowest planetary mass in sample; affects gravity and escape dynamics
- Exceptionally large scale height makes transit chord exceptionally large
- Scale height estimate uses mean molecular weight ~2.5 amu (H₂O-influenced)
- Water abundance intermediate between cool and hot hot Jupiters
- Kepler discovery; star may be fainter than WASP systems (affects observations)
- This system is IDEAL for large-scale-height spectroscopy
