# WASP-18b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-18b is an ultra-hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 2500 K) with mass 10.3 M_J and radius 1.165 R_J, representing the most massive hot Jupiter known. Despite its extreme mass, it maintains a relatively compact size, indicating a massive iron core. With an orbital period of 0.94 days, WASP-18b shows rapid orbital decay and extreme tidal effects.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 95-97%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic species**: H, O, C significant
- **Alkali metals**: Na, K ionized

### Bulk Density: 1.05 g/cm³ (high for its temperature)

### Core Properties

**Core mass**: 80-100 M_⊕ (extremely massive, likely super-Earth size planet core)

**Composition**:
- Iron-nickel center with silicate mantle
- Extensive high-pressure phases
- Post-perovskite mantle at depth

- **Sub-stellar**: 2500-2600 K

### Photochemistry

- **H₂O photodissociation**: τ ~ 100-200 s
- **Atomic H production**: ~10¹¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹

### Clouds and Hazes

**Cloud layers**:
1. **Silicate clouds**: T ~ 1600 K, τ ~ 0.5
2. **TiO haze**: Upper photosphere
3. **Iron oxide**: T < 1400 K

## Summary

WASP-18b is the most massive hot Jupiter known, with an exceptionally large core (80-100 M_⊕) accounting for its higher density despite ultra-hot dayside temperatures (2500 K). Strong gravity (g ~ 90 m/s²) compresses the atmosphere while extreme tidal effects cause rapid orbital decay. CO and alkali metals should be detectable in transmission and emission, providing constraints on atmospheric dynamics in this extreme massive-planet regime.
