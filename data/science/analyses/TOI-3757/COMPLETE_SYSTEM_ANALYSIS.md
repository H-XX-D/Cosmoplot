## 1. Host Star Characterization

TOI-3757 is an M0V (early M-dwarf) with enhanced luminosity compared to later M-types:

**Spectroscopic Parameters:**

- Mass (M*): 0.64 M☉
- Radius (R*): ~0.56 R☉
- Luminosity (L*): 0.087 L☉ (8.7% Solar)
- Surface Gravity (log g): 4.7-4.9

**M-dwarf EUV / UV photochemistry:**
- M-dwarf EUV / soft X-ray photochemistry stellar EUV photochemistry strengthens H₂O/CH₄ opacity
- Photochemical Efficiency: 38-45% of EUV drives molecule-producing reactions
- Effect at Higher T: At 759 K, photochemistry becomes more complex with SO₂ channels

**Stellar Activity:**
- Rotation Period: ~25-40 days (faster than M3V due to higher mass)
- Chromospheric Activity Index: ~0.002-0.004 (moderately active)
- Flare Frequency: 1-3 significant flares per month
- XUV Radiation: Enhanced relative to M3V (higher absolute flux)

### XUV Radiation Environment

## 4. Atmospheric Composition and Chemistry

### Primary Constituents

**Major Components:**
- H₂: 68-72% (primordial)
- He: 22-28% (primordial)
- H₂O: 0.5-2% (condensing, but only at cloud level ~300 K)
- CH₄: 1-3% (enhanced by the M-dwarf's strong UV field at high temperature)
- SO₂: 0.1-1% (activated at this higher temperature!)
- H₂S: <0.1% (trace, reducing agent)

### Sulfur Chemistry Activation

**Key Feature of TOI-3757b**: At 759 K, SULFUR CHEMISTRY BECOMES ACTIVE

This is not present in cooler TOI-1231b (329.6 K) but dominates hot TOI-3757b:

**Sulfur Reactions (Temperature-Dependent):**
1. H₂S + OH → SH + H₂O (slow at <500 K, FAST at 700+ K)
2. SH + O₂ → SO + OH (rapid chain reaction at high T)
3. SO + OH → SO₂ + H (final oxidation)
4. SO₂ + hν → SO + O (photolysis)
5. SO + HO₂ → SO₂ + OH (recycling)

**Result**: SO₂ abundance ~0.3-1% (observable in transmission spectroscopy!)

### Temperature-Dependent Photochemistry

**At 759 K (compared to 329.6 K of TOI-1231b):**
- CH₄ lifetime: 10 years (vs 100 years at lower T)
- CO formation: Rapid (1-10 ppm/month)
- HCN formation: Possible at detectable levels
- Tholins: Fast formation (months to years)
- Overall photochemical timescale: 10-100× faster

## 7. Photochemistry (M-Dwarf at High Temperature)

### Temperature-Dependent Transition

At 759 K, TOI-3757b sits in the **PHOTOCHEMICALLY ACTIVE** regime where:
- Molecular timescales: days-to-weeks
- Radical timescales: milliseconds-to-seconds
- Chemical networks: Complex branching

### M-dwarf UV photochemistry at high temperature

**Same strong UV as cooler TOI-1231b, but effects are different:**

**Water Chemistry (same as cooler planets):**
- H₂O + hν → H + OH (photolysis remains primary)
- OH recycling still occurs (favorable thermodynamics)
- Result: Moderate H₂O abundance (0.5-2%)

**Methane Enhanced Chemistry:**
- CH₄ + hν → CH₃ + H (faster photolysis at higher T and higher EUV)
- CH₄ + OH → CH₃ + H₂O (rapid at 759 K)
- CH₃ + HO₂ → CH₄ + O₂ (regeneration is fast)
- Result: Higher CH₄ than pure oxidation would predict (1-3%)

**New Sulfur Chemistry (Temperature-Enabled):**
- H₂S oxidation via OH is now RAPID (k doubles every 100 K above 500 K)
- SO₂ production rate: ~10⁸ molecules/(cm² s) (STRONG)
- SO₂ lifetime: ~1000 years (accumulates in atmosphere)
- SO₂ abundance: 0.3-1% (OBSERVABLE)

**Photochemical Product Distribution:**
- H₂O: 0.5-2% (abundant, recycled)
- CH₄: 1-3% (enhanced, longer lifetime)
- CO: 10-100 ppm (moderate production from CH₄)
- SO₂: 300-1000 ppm (NEW, unique to hot planets)
- HCN: 0.1-1 ppm (possible at high T)
