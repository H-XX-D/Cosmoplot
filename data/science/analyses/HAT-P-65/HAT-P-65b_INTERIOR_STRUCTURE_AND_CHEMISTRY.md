# HAT-P-65b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

HAT-P-65b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1750 K) with mass 1.89 M_J and radius 1.46 R_J orbiting an F-star every 2.61 days. This moderately massive hot Jupiter exhibits strong water vapor absorption and represents the transition zone where methane becomes a significant atmospheric component.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2% (significant absorption)
- **Methane**: ~0.1-0.3%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic H**: 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³
- **Alkali metals**: Na, K present

### Bulk Density: 0.55 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 20-25 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 1750 |
| 0.8 | 15 | 1500 |
| 0.6 | 150 | 1250 |
| 0.4 | 1,500 | 1000 |
| 0.2 | 15,000 | 800 |
| 0.0 | 150,000 | 700 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~1750 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar**: 1750-1850 K
- **Terminator**: 1450-1650 K
- **Limb**: 1250-1450 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.76-0.84 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H₂O | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| CO | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |
| Na⁺ | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |

### Photochemistry

- **H₂O photodissociation**: τ ~ 300-1000 s
- **CH₄ partial dissociation**: ~10-30% dissociated
- **Atomic H production**: ~10¹⁰ cm⁻² s⁻¹

### Clouds and Hazes

**Cloud layers**:
1. **Silicate clouds**: T ~ 1500 K
2. **Water cloud base**: Forming at ~1400 K (limb only)
3. **Upper hazes**: TiO/VO

## Nightside Chemistry (~1200 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar**: 1200-1300 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 450-650 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance | Ratio |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| H₂O | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.2-0.5 |
| CO | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.5-1.0 |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 1.0-3.0 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁵ | 100 |

### Thermal Chemistry

**Reaction timescales**:
- Hydrogen recombination: τ ~ 50-200 s
- Water formation: τ ~ 10-50 s
- Methane stabilization: τ ~ 1-10 hours

### Condensates

- **Silicate clouds**: Extended to nightside
- **Water clouds**: Forming on cool nightside (1200-1400 K)
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 0.5-1.5

## Circulation & Wind Patterns

### Wind Speeds

- **Equatorial jets**: 2-4 km/s
- **Meridional circulation**: 0.5-1.5 km/s
- **Vertical motion**: 5-15 m/s
- **Advection period**: 14-18 hours

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (1750 K)
- **1-2 μm**: H₂O bands visible
- **3.2 μm**: Strong H₂O feature
- **4.7 μm**: CO fundamental (60-150 ppm)
- **8 μm**: CH₄ bands

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 60-150 ppm
- **Water vapor**: Strong features (100-300 ppm)
- **Alkali metals**: Na (20-60 ppm), K (8-20 ppm)

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1.1 μm | 120-200 | H₂O |
| 3.2 μm | 150-250 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 120-200 | CO |
| 8 μm | 80-150 | CH₄ |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: Multiple bands, SNR ~3-5σ
2. **CO**: 4.7 μm, SNR ~2-4σ
3. **CH₄**: 8 μm, SNR ~1-2σ
4. **Na**: 589 nm, SNR ~2-4σ

---

## Summary

HAT-P-65b is a hot Jupiter where water vapor becomes a dominant feature. Dayside temperatures of 1750 K allow methane to persist alongside CO and H₂O. The 450-650 K day-night temperature difference drives moderate circulation. JWST will easily detect water vapor in both transmission and emission, along with CO and CH₄, providing a complete picture of carbon-oxygen chemistry at this temperature regime.
