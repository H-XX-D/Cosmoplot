# KELT-20b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

KELT-20b is an ultra-hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 3700 K) with mass 3.18 M_J and radius 1.69 R_J, orbiting an F-type star every 3.47 days. This massive ultra-hot Jupiter bridges between the extreme KELT-9b and typical hot Jupiters, exhibiting strong alkali metal ionization and extensive molecular dissociation.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2% (photodissociated)
- **Methane**: <0.1% (dissociated above 1500 K)
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic species**: H, O, C significant
- **Alkali metals**: Na, K heavily ionized on dayside
- **Trace metals**: Fe, Ti, Mg ionized

### Bulk Density

**Mean density**: 0.66 g/cm³

### Core Properties

**Core mass**: 20-30 M_⊕

**High-pressure phases**:
- Metallic hydrogen layer
- Post-perovskite silicates
- Iron-nickel core (HCP phase likely)

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |

| 1.0 | 1 | 3700 |
| 0.8 | 100 | 3100 |
| 0.6 | 1,000 | 2400 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~3700 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar**: 3700-3800 K
- **Terminator**: 3300-3500 K
- **Limb**: 3000-3200 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.75-0.85 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² |
| O (atomic) | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |
| CO | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |
| Na⁺ | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |
| K⁺ | 10⁻⁷ - 10⁻⁶ |

### Photochemistry

**Ionization/dissociation processes**:
- H₂O photodissociation: τ ~ 50-100 s
- Metal ionization: Na⁺/K⁺ formation rates high
- Atomic H production: 10¹¹-10¹² cm⁻² s⁻¹ escape flux

### Clouds and Hazes

**Condensate layers**:
1. **Corundum (Al₂O₃)**: T ~ 2000 K
2. **Iron oxide**: T ~ 1700 K
3. **Upper haze**: TiO, VO opacity

## Nightside Chemistry (~2900 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar**: 2900-3000 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 700-900 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Day/Night Ratio |
|---------|-----------------|
| H₂O | 0.1 |
| CO | 0.1-0.2 |
| H (atomic) | 100 |
| Na | 10-20 |

### Thermal Chemistry

**Recombination**:
- H + H + M → H₂ + M; τ ~ 100-500 s
- Water formation: τ ~ 10-100 s
- CO stabilization

### Wind Speeds

- **Equatorial jets**: 5-8 km/s
- **Vertical updrafts**: 20-40 m/s
- **Advection time**: 8-12 hours around planet

### Circulation Structure

- Strong equatorial super-rotation
- Vigorous day-night circulation
- High Rayleigh number convection

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (3700 K)
- **1-2 μm**: Strong Na, weak H₂O
- **4.7 μm**: CO fundamental (100-250 ppm)

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: τ ~ 0.5-1.5
- **Alkali metals**: Na (50-100 ppm), K (20-50 ppm)
- **Molecular features**: CO <20 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) |
|-----------|------------|
| 1 μm | 200-350 |
| 4.7 μm | 250-400 |
| 11 μm | 150-250 |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **CO**: 4.7 μm, SNR ~4-6σ
2. **Na**: 589 nm, SNR ~5-8σ
3. **K**: 769 nm, SNR ~2-4σ
4. **H₂O**: Marginal, SNR ~1-2σ

## Summary

KELT-20b is a massive ultra-hot Jupiter with extreme dayside temperatures (3700 K) and significant alkali metal ionization. Strong day-night temperature gradient (700-900 K) drives vigorous wind circulation at 5-8 km/s. CO should be easily detectable at 4.7 μm, along with Na and K lines. Phase curve measurements will constrain atmospheric circulation and dynamics. This world represents the most massive ultra-hot Jupiter currently characterized.
