# TOI-270b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

TOI-270b is a cool sub-Neptune (Teq ≈ 530 K) with mass 3.2 M_E and radius 2.4 R_E orbiting a K-star every 3.35 days. First member of compact multi-planet system, TOI-270b exhibits pronounced molecular features with significant ammonia and methane across day-night terminator.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 60-75% (substantial core)
- **Water vapor**: ~8-15%
- **Methane**: ~3-6%
- **Ammonia**: ~2-4%
- **Nitrogen**: ~10-15%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.1-0.2%

### Bulk Density: ~0.7 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 12-15 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Water vapor**: 400-800 ppm (ice cloud modulation)
- **Methane**: 200-400 ppm
- **Ammonia**: 150-300 ppm
- **Continuum**: Elevated from particles

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1 μm | 250-500 | Continuum |
| 3.2 μm | 350-700 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 300-600 | CO + H₂O |
| 8 μm | 350-700 | CH₄ |
| 10 μm | 250-500 | NH₃ |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~8-15σ (very strong)
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~4-8σ
3. **NH₃**: SNR ~3-7σ
4. **N₂**: SNR ~2-4σ (nitrogen detection possible)

## Summary

TOI-270b is the primary member of a compact three-planet system exhibiting extreme molecular richness at cool temperatures. Small day-night temperature difference (60-110 K) creates well-mixed chemistry. Extensive water and ammonia ice clouds dominate transmission spectrum with 400-800 ppm features. Detection of multiple molecular species including nitrogen represents opportunity to characterize complete atmospheric composition. This system exemplifies how multi-planet systems provide comparative planetology insights.
