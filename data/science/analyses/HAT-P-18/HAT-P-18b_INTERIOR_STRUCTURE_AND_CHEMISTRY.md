# HAT-P-18b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

HAT-P-18b is a warm hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 900 K) with mass 0.195 M_J and radius 1.188 R_J orbiting a G-type star every 5.51 days. One of the lowest-mass hydrogen-rich planets, HAT-P-18b shows strong molecular features with prominent water and methane absorption.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 92-96%
- **Water vapor**: ~2-5%
- **Methane**: ~1-2%
- **Ammonia**: ~0.5-1.5%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.2-0.5%

### Bulk Density: 0.10 g/cm³ (extremely inflated)

### Core Mass: 8-12 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 0.5 | 900 |
| 0.8 | 3 | 850 |
| 0.6 | 30 | 750 |
| 0.4 | 300 | 650 |
| 0.2 | 3,000 | 550 |

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 120-250 ppm
- **Water vapor**: 250-450 ppm
- **Methane**: 150-300 ppm
- **Ammonia**: 80-150 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) |
|-----------|------------|
| 3.2 μm | 150-300 |
| 4.7 μm | 150-300 |
| 8 μm | 180-350 |
| 10 μm | 100-200 |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~6-10σ
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~4-8σ
3. **NH₃**: SNR ~2-4σ
4. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ

---

## Summary

HAT-P-18b is an extremely low-mass hydrogen-rich planet with massive extended atmosphere. Warm dayside temperature (900 K) enables detection of water, methane, and ammonia in high SNR. Extended H/He envelope creates prominent molecular features ideal for JWST characterization of warm sub-Jupiter atmospheres.
