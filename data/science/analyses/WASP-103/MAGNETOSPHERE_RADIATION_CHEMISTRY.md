## PART 4: PHOTOCHEMICAL REACTION NETWORK (MINIMAL)

### 4.1 Water: Beyond Dissociation

**H₂O Status at 2508 K:**
Not just dissociated, but atomic:
```
H₂O + hν → H + OH (first dissociation)
H + hν → H⁺ + e⁻ (ionization)
OH + hν → O + H (second dissociation)
O + hν → O⁺ + e⁻ (ionization)
```

**Result:** Essentially ZERO H₂O molecules; atmosphere is atomic H, O, He plasma.

### 4.2 CO: Molecular Species at Extreme Conditions

Even CO is partially broken down:
```
CO + hν → C + O (complete photodissociation)
C + hν → C⁺ + e⁻ (ionization of carbon)
```

x(CO) < 10⁻³ (mostly atomic carbon and oxygen)

### 4.3 Atomic Species Dominance

At 2508 K in the upper atmosphere:
- **H atoms:** Dominant species, n(H) ≈ 10⁸-10⁹ cm⁻³
- **O atoms:** Significant, n(O) ≈ 10⁷-10⁸ cm⁻³
- **H⁺ ions:** Abundant, n(H⁺) ≈ 10⁷ cm⁻³
- **e⁻ electrons:** n_e ≈ 10⁷ cm⁻³ (from ionization)
- **He, C atoms:** Trace amounts

**Escape flux:** Φ_hydro ≈ 10⁷-10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (MAXIMUM in sample)

### 5.3 Photochemical Escape
