# WASP-103b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-103b is an ultra-hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 2500 K) with a mass of 1.49 M_J and radius of 1.44 R_J, orbiting an F-type star every 0.925 days. This extremely inflated gas giant provides a case study in atmospheric expansion and dissociation of complex molecules at extreme temperatures.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor (H₂O)**: ~1% (significantly photodissociated)
- **Methane (CH₄)**: <0.1% (mostly dissociated above 1500 K)
- **Carbon monoxide (CO)**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic species**: H, O, C present
- **Trace metals**: Na, K, Fe (partially ionized)

### Bulk Density and Composition Profile

**Mean bulk density**: 0.52 g/cm³ (one of least dense known gas giants)

**Composition layers**:
1. **Thermosphere (P < 10 mbar)**: Atomic hydrogen, helium, ionized metals
2. **Upper photosphere (10-100 mbar)**: Alkali metals ionized, H₂O dissociation zone
3. **Middle photosphere (100-1000 mbar)**: H₂/He transition, complex chemistry
4. **Lower envelope (>1000 mbar)**: H₂/He dominated, cloud base region

### Core Mass and Composition

**Estimated core mass**: 20-30 M_⊕ (heavy for its mass)

**Core structure**:
- Iron-nickel center with silicate mantle
- Higher density profile than Jupiter
- Extensive high-pressure phases

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) | State |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 2500 | Gas/plasma |
| 0.8 | 100 | 2100 | H₂ molecular |
| 0.6 | 1,000 | 1600 | Metallic H transition |
| 0.4 | 10,000 | 1200 | Metallic hydrogen |
| 0.2 | 100,000 | 900 | Mantle |
| 0.0 | 1,000,000 | 800 | Iron core |

## Dayside Chemistry (Hot Side, ~2500 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar point**: 2500-2600 K
- **Terminator**: 2200-2400 K
- **Limb**: 2000-2200 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.76-0.84 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |
| CO | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| H₂O | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |
| Na⁺ | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |

### Photochemistry

- **H₂O photodissociation timescale**: 100-500 s
- **CO formation**: Rapid recombination
- **Alkali metal ionization**: Strong on dayside
- **Atomic H production**: ~10⁻³ mixing ratio

### Clouds and Hazes

**Proposed condensate layers**:

1. **Silicate clouds**: T ~ 1600 K
   - Mg₂SiO₄ (forsterite) formation
   - Altitude: 100-1000 mbar

2. **Metal oxide clouds**: T < 1400 K
   - Al₂O₃, TiO₂ possible
   - Altitude: 1-10 bar

3. **High-altitude aerosols**: τ_haze ~ 0.5-1.5

## Nightside Chemistry (Cool Side, ~1900 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar point**: 1900-2000 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 500-700 K
- **Lapse rate**: ~0.15 K/km

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Nightside Abundance | Day/Night Ratio |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| H₂O | 10⁻⁴ | 0.1 |
| CO | 10⁻⁴ | 0.1 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁵ | 100 |
| Na | 10⁻⁶ | 10 |

### Condensates

- **Iron oxide clouds**: ~1400 K condensation
- **Silicate clouds**: More abundant on nightside
- **Optical depth**: τ_cloud ~ 0.5-1.0

## Circulation & Wind Patterns

### Wind Speeds

- **Equatorial jets**: 3-5 km/s
- **Meridional circulation**: 0.5-2 km/s
- **Vertical updrafts**: 5-20 m/s
- **Advection timescale**: 10-15 hours around planet

### Circulation Structure

1. **Equatorial super-rotation**: Driven by thermal gradient
2. **Meridional cells**: Day-night circulation
3. **Wave activity**: Significant eddy contribution

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (2500 K blackbody)
- **1-2 μm**: Na/K lines, weak H₂O
- **4.7 μm**: CO fundamental (100-300 ppm)
- **Peak wavelength**: ~1200 nm

### Transmission Spectrum

**Key features**:

1. **Rayleigh haze**: τ ~ 0.5-1.5 (100-300 ppm elevation)
2. **Alkali metals**: Na (30-100 ppm), K (10-30 ppm)
3. **Molecular features**: CO <20 ppm, H₂O <10 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1.5 μm | 150-300 | Continuum |
| 4.7 μm | 200-400 | CO + H₂O |
| 11 μm | 100-200 | Continuum |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **CO**: 4.7 μm, SNR ~3-5σ
2. **H₂O**: 2-3 μm, SNR ~2-3σ
3. **Na**: 589 nm, SNR ~3-5σ
4. **K**: 769 nm, SNR <2σ

### Best Observation Strategy

1. **Phase curve**: F444W (4.4 μm) for CO emission
2. **Transmission**: NIRSpec G395M for spectroscopy
3. **Focus on**: CO detection, phase offset measurement
4. **Program time**: 30-40 hours

## Summary

WASP-103b is an extremely inflated ultra-hot Jupiter with low density and strong atmospheric expansion. Its massive atmosphere (reaching far from the planet core) makes it excellent for transmission spectroscopy. The dayside chemistry is dominated by photodissociation products (atomic H, O), while the nightside shows molecular recombination. CO should be detectable with moderate SNR, while alkali metals provide strong transmission features. The enormous temperature contrast (600-700 K) between day and night drives vigorous circulation patterns.
