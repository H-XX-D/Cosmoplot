CHALLENGES & OPPORTUNITIES
================================================================================
Challenges:
  - EXTREMELY short orbital period (rapid observations needed, timing critical)
  - Extreme photochemistry difficult to model (non-equilibrium possible)
  - Hydrogen escape may be non-hydrodynamic; complex dynamics
  - Tidal heating uncertain; coupled atmosphere-interior modeling needed

Opportunities:
  - UNIQUE extreme laboratory for atmospheric physics limits
  - Clear TiO/VO detection expected (very strong opacity)
  - Tests atmospheric chemistry models at absolute limits
  - Hydrogen escape mechanisms directly observable (maximum rates)
  - Largest transmission features expected in sample (except Kepler-12b scale height)

NOTES & CAVEATS
================================================================================
- Hottest temperature in 7-planet sample; approaches atomic hydrogen escape limit
- F8V host produces intense UV; combined with short orbit = extreme insolation
- Orbital period (0.926 days) SHORTEST in sample; extreme tidal effects
- Mass (1.494 M_J) HEAVIEST in sample; affects gravity and scale height
- Tidal heating magnitude uncertain; significant but hard to quantify
- Hydrogen escape rate at theoretical maximum; possible non-equilibrium states
- 3D modeling essential; 1D approximations may break down
- Young age (<1 Gyr) suggests system recently migrated to ultra-close orbit
