# HAT-P-32b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

HAT-P-32b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1900 K) with mass 0.947 M_J and radius 1.789 R_J, exhibiting moderate atmosphere expansion. With an orbital period of 2.15 days around an F-star, HAT-P-32b represents a transition between ultra-hot and typical hot Jupiters with significant molecular absorption features.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Methane**: ~0.1-0.3% (some present, partially dissociated)
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic species**: H, O present but reduced vs. ultra-hot
- **Alkali metals**: Na, K present, some ionization

### Bulk Density: 0.32 g/cm³ (inflated)

### Core Properties

**Core mass**: 15-20 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |

| 1.0 | 1 | 1900 |
| 0.8 | 20 | 1600 |
| 0.6 | 200 | 1300 |
| 0.4 | 2,000 | 1000 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~1900 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar**: 1900-2000 K
- **Terminator**: 1600-1800 K
- **Limb**: 1400-1600 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.76-0.84 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |

| CO | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| Na⁺ | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |

### Photochemistry

- **H₂O photodissociation**: τ ~ 200-500 s
- **Atomic H production**: ~10¹⁰ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- **CH₄ partial dissociation**: ~20-50% dissociated

### Clouds and Hazes

**Cloud layers**:
1. **Silicate clouds**: T ~ 1600 K, Mg₂SiO₄
2. **TiO haze**: Upper photosphere
3. **Sulfur aerosols**: Possible

## Nightside Chemistry (~1400 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar**: 1400-1500 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 400-600 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance | Ratio |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| H₂O | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.1-0.2 |
| CO | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.5-1.0 |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 1.0-2.0 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁵ | 50-100 |
| Na | 10⁻⁶ | 10 |

### Thermal Chemistry

**Recombination**:
- H + H + M → H₂ + M; τ ~ 100-500 s
- Water formation: τ ~ 20-100 s
- Methane stabilization

### Condensates

- **Silicate clouds**: More abundant on nightside
- **Water ice possible**: In deepest cold regions
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 0.5-1.0

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (1900 K)
- **1-2 μm**: Na lines visible, weak H₂O
- **2-3 μm**: CH₄ bands weak
- **4.7 μm**: CO fundamental (80-200 ppm)

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 80-200 ppm elevation
- **Alkali metals**: Na (30-80 ppm), K (10-30 ppm)
- **Molecular features**: CO, H₂O, CH₄ all detectable (weak)

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1 μm | 150-250 | Continuum |
| 3.2 μm | 100-200 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 150-250 | CO |
| 8 μm | 100-200 | CH₄ |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **CO**: 4.7 μm, SNR ~2-4σ
2. **H₂O**: 3.2 μm, SNR ~2-3σ
3. **CH₄**: 8 μm, SNR ~1-2σ
4. **Na**: 589 nm, SNR ~3-5σ

## Summary

HAT-P-32b is a highly inflated hot Jupiter (density 0.32 g/cm³) with significant molecular absorption features. Dayside temperatures of 1900 K preserve some methane, allowing for detection of three different carbon-bearing molecules (H₂O, CO, CH₄). The 400-600 K day-night temperature difference drives moderate winds of 3-5 km/s. JWST can detect CO and H₂O simultaneously, probing the transition between ultra-hot and cooler hot Jupiter chemistries.
