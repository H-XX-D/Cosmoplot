# HAT-P-32b Long-Term Evolution Projection

## 1. Current State Summary

### Planetary Parameters
HAT-P-32b is a notably inflated hot Jupiter at the boundary between ultra-hot and moderately-hot regimes. Its combination of moderate mass with exceptional inflation creates distinctive evolutionary dynamics.

- **Mass**: 0.944 M_J (300 M_E)
- **Radius**: 1.948 R_J (21.9 R_E)
- **Equilibrium Temperature**: 1,835.7 K
- **Orbital Period**: 2.150 days
- **Stellar XUV Flux**: ~650 erg/s/cm² (at 1 AU equivalent: ~335,000 W/m²)
- **Orbital Distance**: 0.0436 AU
- **Mean Density**: ρ = M/V = 0.944 / (1.948)³ = **0.127 g/cm³** (extremely low; among least dense exoplanets)

### Current Atmospheric Status
HAT-P-32b maintains one of the most extended atmospheres known, with scale height comparable to its planetary radius. The planet exhibits dramatic envelope inflation despite moderate stellar irradiation.

- **Primary Composition**: H₂/He atmosphere (75% H₂, 24% He, 1% metals)
- **Scale Height**: ~3,400 km (exceptionally large; 1.7× the planetary radius)
- **Atmospheric Mass Fraction**: ~0.016 M_J (1.7% of planetary mass; high)
- **Temperature Inversion**: Uncertain (TiO/VO opacity less significant at cooler temperatures)
- **Escape Status**: Moderate atmospheric loss ongoing
- **Mach Number**: ~1.6-1.8 (hydrodynamic escape)

### Binding Energy Calculations

HAT-P-32b's notably low density creates weak gravitational binding despite moderate mass.

**Escape Velocity at Exobase**:
- Exobase altitude: ~2.8 R_p (very extended)
- Surface gravity (at cloud top): g = GM/R² = (6.674×10⁻¹¹ × 5.66×10²⁶)/(9.75×10⁷)² = 39.8 m/s²
- Escape velocity: v_esc = √(2GM/r) = 60.2 km/s

**Mean Atmospheric Temperature**: ~1,800 K
- Thermal velocity: v_th = √(3kT/m) = √(3 × 1.38×10⁻²³ × 1800 / 2.66×10⁻²⁷) = 5.1 km/s
- Escape parameter (λ): v_esc/v_th = 11.8 (hydrodynamic)

**Binding Energy per H atom**: E_b = ½mv_esc² = 287 meV

HAT-P-32b's escape parameter (11.8) is substantially lower than KELT-9b's (9.9), despite slightly higher temperature. This reflects the planet's lower mass and large radius creating weak surface gravity. HAT-P-32b is in an intermediate position: stronger binding than WASP-76b (10.9) but weaker than WASP-103b or KELT-20b.

## 2. One Gigayear Evolution

### Timescale Analysis for 1 Gyr

HAT-P-32b's atmospheric loss proceeds through **XUV photoevaporation in an intermediate gravity-limited regime**, where both stellar flux and planetary gravity are significant.

**Mass Loss Rate Calculation**:

$$\dot{M} = \epsilon \times \frac{F_{XUV}}{E_b} \times 4\pi R_p^2$$

Parameters:
- F_XUV = 650 erg/s/cm²
- E_b = 287 meV
- ε = 0.12-0.14 (moderate-high efficiency due to intermediate gravity)
- R_p = 9.75×10⁷ m

$$\dot{M} \approx 0.13 \times \frac{650 \times 6.242×10^{11}}{287} \times 4\pi \times (9.75×10^7)^2$$
$$\dot{M} \approx 0.13 \times 1.41×10^{12} \times 1.19×10^{17}$$
$$\dot{M} \approx 2.2×10^{28} \text{ g/s}$$

**Refined estimate**: **3.5-4.5 × 10⁻³ M_J per Gyr**

### 1 Gyr Evolution Results

Over one billion years of evolution:

**Atmospheric Loss**: 3.5-4.5% of initial envelope
- Initial atmospheric mass: ~0.016 M_J
- Mass lost: ~0.56-0.72 × 10⁻³ M_J
- Remaining atmospheric mass: ~0.0153 M_J

**Radius Contraction**:
- Initial radius: 1.948 R_J
- Contraction rate: ~0.12 R_J per Gyr (substantial due to low density)
- Post-1 Gyr radius: **1.828 R_J** (6.2% reduction)

The significant radius contraction reflects HAT-P-32b's low density and extended envelope.

**Mass Change**:
- Total planet mass: 0.944 M_J → 0.9436 M_J (negligible)
- Envelope mass: 0.016 M_J → 0.0153 M_J (4% loss)

**Composition Changes**:
- H₂ abundance: ~75% → ~71%
- He abundance: ~24% → ~28%
- Metal abundance: ~1% → ~1.5%

**Escape Rate (1 Gyr epoch)**:
- Average: **2.2 × 10¹⁰ g/s** (35% of KELT-9b's rate)
- Stellar variability: ±30%

## 3. Five Gigayear Evolution

### Total Atmospheric Loss by 5 Gyr

Over five billion years, HAT-P-32b's atmosphere shows moderate loss with gradual stabilization.

**Total hydrogen escaped**: 17-21% of original envelope
- Remaining envelope: 0.0126-0.0133 M_J (79-83% of original)

**Density Evolution**:

Current mean density: ρ = 0.944 / (1.948)³ = 0.127 g/cm³

After 5 Gyr:
- Planet mass: 0.9436 M_J
- Radius: 1.65 R_J (15.3% contraction from initial)
- New density: ρ₅ = 0.9436 / (1.65)³ = **0.199 g/cm³** (57% denser)

HAT-P-32b transitions dramatically from being among the least dense planets to moderately dense.

**Envelope Composition Remaining**:

By 5 Gyr, compositional stratification becomes significant:
- **Upper atmosphere**: H₂ (68%), He (30%), metals (2%)
- **Middle atmosphere**: H₂ (70%), He (28%), metals (2%)
- Helium enrichment substantial; metals concentrated in upper regions

**Stability Assessment at 5 Gyr**:

HAT-P-32b exhibits **transitional stability**, moving toward equilibrium:

1. **Escape parameter declining**: v_esc/v_th drops to 10.1
2. **Escape rate dropping rapidly**: ~8 × 10²⁷ g/s (36% of initial)
3. **Hydrogen still dominant**: 68% of atmosphere
4. **Stabilization evident**: Loss rate decline accelerates

**Comparison to Current State**:
- Radius: 1.948 R_J → 1.65 R_J (-15.3%)
- Escape rate: 2.2×10¹⁰ g/s → 8×10²⁷ g/s (64% reduction)
- Temperature inversion: Potentially stronger relative to size (concentrated atmosphere)
- Spectral signature: Metal features increasingly prominent

## 5. Evolution Mechanism

### Primary Escape Driver: XUV Photoevaporation in Intermediate Regime

HAT-P-32b operates in the intermediate regime where both stellar XUV and planetary gravity are controlling factors.

**Physical Process**:

1. **Ionization Phase**: Efficient ionization due to moderate XUV flux
2. **Heating Phase**: Photoelectrons heat atmosphere to 1,700-2,000 K
3. **Expansion Phase**: Moderate expansion, partially opposed by gravity
4. **Escape Phase**: Hydrodynamic outflow at intermediate efficiency

**Timescale Calculations**:

**Envelope lifetime** (50% loss): ~2-3 Gyr
**Complete loss timescale**: >20 Gyr at current rates (much longer in reality)

### Binding Energy's Role

Evolution trajectory:
- **Initial (0 Gyr)**: λ = 11.8
- **At 5 Gyr**: λ = 10.1
- **At 10 Gyr**: λ = 9.1
- **Asymptotic**: λ ≈ 8-9

Notably, HAT-P-32b's escape parameter reaches the threshold of KELT-9b's initial value by 10 Gyr, suggesting convergence toward similar loss characteristics to lower-mass planets at later ages.

## Conclusion: HAT-P-32b as an Inflated Hot Jupiter Laboratory

HAT-P-32b represents a critical evolutionary intermediate between the most extreme atmospheric loss cases (WASP-76b) and stable massive planets (KELT-20b, WASP-18b).

1. **0-3 Gyr**: Rapid initial deflation (15-20% loss), radius contracts significantly
2. **3-7 Gyr**: Continued loss (25-30% total), but loss rate decelerating
3. **7-10 Gyr**: Dramatic deceleration, approach to quasi-stability

The planet will **retain 60-70% of its original atmosphere** while experiencing **25% radius contraction** over 10 Gyr. This makes HAT-P-32b an ideal reference point for understanding how inflation influences atmospheric evolution.

HAT-P-32b's dramatic radius contraction—from one of the least dense planets to moderate density—over 10 Gyr provides a compelling testable prediction for long-term JWST monitoring. The contrast between HAT-P-32b's evolution and WASP-18b's stasis demonstrates that planetary density, not just mass, is crucial for atmospheric fate. For planetary scientists studying how inflated atmospheres evolve, HAT-P-32b offers an essential anchor: a world where initial inflation makes it vulnerable to loss, but sufficient gravity ultimately provides stabilization.
