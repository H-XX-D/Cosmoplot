# WASP-52b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-52b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1567 K) with mass 0.46 M_J and radius 1.27 R_J orbiting a G-type star every 1.75 days. Low-mass hot Jupiter with balanced molecular features and strong transmission spectrum from extended hydrogen-helium envelope.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Methane**: ~0.2-0.4%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic H**: 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴

### Bulk Density: 0.23 g/cm³ (inflated)

### Core Mass: 12-15 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|

| 0.8 | 10 | 1350 |
| 0.6 | 100 | 1150 |

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 70-150 ppm (extended atmosphere)
- **Water vapor**: 150-350 ppm (strong)
- **Methane**: 60-130 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) |
|-----------|------------|
| 3.2 μm | 100-200 |
| 4.7 μm | 80-160 |
| 8 μm | 100-180 |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~4-6σ
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~2-4σ
3. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ

---

## Summary

WASP-52b is a low-mass hot Jupiter with extended atmosphere and strong molecular features. Balanced H₂O/CO/CH₄ chemistry creates rich transmission spectrum ideal for JWST characterization. Extended atmosphere enhances signal-to-noise in transmission spectroscopy observations.
