# WASP-19b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-19b is an ultra-hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 2650 K) with mass 1.11 M_J and radius 1.37 R_J, in a close orbit every 0.79 days around a G-type star. This planet exhibits strong orbital decay and represents a transition between ultra-hot and regular hot Jupiters with moderate mass loss.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Methane**: <0.1%
- **Atomic species**: H, O, C present
- **Alkali metals**: Na, K ionized

### Bulk Density: 0.60 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 18-25 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 2650 |
| 0.8 | 50 | 2200 |
| 0.6 | 500 | 1700 |
| 0.4 | 5,000 | 1200 |
| 0.2 | 50,000 | 1000 |
| 0.0 | 500,000 | 900 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~2650 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar point**: 2650-2750 K
- **Terminator**: 2300-2500 K
- **Limb**: 2000-2200 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.76-0.84 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² |
| CO | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| Na⁺ | 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁵ |
| K⁺ | 10⁻⁷ - 10⁻⁶ |

### Photochemistry

- **H₂O dissociation**: τ ~ 100-200 s
- **Atomic H escape**: ~10¹¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- **Alkali ionization**: τ ~ 10-100 s

### Clouds and Hazes

**Cloud layers**:
1. **Silicate clouds**: T ~ 1600 K, Mg₂SiO₄
2. **TiO haze**: Upper photosphere
3. **Iron oxide**: T < 1500 K

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **0.5-1 μm**: Thermal continuum (2650 K)
- **1-2 μm**: Na lines, weak features
- **4.7 μm**: CO fundamental (100-250 ppm)
- **Peak wavelength**: ~1100 nm

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 100-300 ppm elevation
- **Alkali metals**: Na (40-100 ppm), K (15-40 ppm)
- **Molecular bands**: CO <20 ppm, H₂O <10 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1 μm | 180-300 | Continuum |
| 4.7 μm | 200-350 | CO + H₂O |
| 11 μm | 150-250 | Continuum |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **CO**: 4.7 μm, SNR ~3-5σ
2. **H₂O**: 2-3 μm, SNR ~1-2σ
3. **Na**: 589 nm, SNR ~4-6σ
4. **K**: 769 nm, SNR ~2-3σ

---

## Summary

WASP-19b is an ultra-hot Jupiter showing strong atmospheric expansion and rapid orbital decay. Dayside temperatures of 2650 K drive significant water dissociation and alkali metal ionization. The 600-850 K day-night temperature difference creates strong circulation with equatorial winds of 4-6 km/s. CO should be detectable at 4.7 μm with good SNR, while Na and K alkali lines appear in both transmission and emission. This system offers excellent opportunities to study atmospheric mass loss and dynamics in close-in gas giants.
