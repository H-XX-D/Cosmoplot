# WASP-19b Long-Term Evolution Projection

## 1. Current State Summary

### Planetary Parameters
WASP-19b is an ultra-hot Jupiter notable for its moderately inflated radius combined with substantial mass. It represents a transition between the most extreme ultra-hots and more moderate hot Jupiters, making it an excellent case study for mid-range atmospheric loss.

- **Mass**: 1.165 M_J (370 M_E)
- **Radius**: 1.634 R_J (18.4 R_E)
- **Equilibrium Temperature**: 2,113 K
- **Orbital Period**: 0.788 days
- **Stellar XUV Flux**: ~1,100 erg/s/cm² (at 1 AU equivalent: ~570,000 W/m²)
- **Orbital Distance**: 0.0165 AU

### Current Atmospheric Status
WASP-19b maintains an H/He atmosphere with potential TiO/VO opacity features creating temperature inversion. The planet exhibits moderate atmospheric escape characteristics.

- **Primary Composition**: H₂/He atmosphere (75% H₂, 24% He, 1% metals)
- **Scale Height**: ~2,600 km (moderately extended)
- **Atmospheric Mass Fraction**: ~0.010 M_J (0.86% of planetary mass)
- **Temperature Inversion**: Likely present (TiO/VO opacity significant)
- **Escape Status**: Moderate atmospheric loss ongoing
- **Mach Number**: ~1.7-1.95 (hydrodynamic escape regime)

### Binding Energy Calculations

WASP-19b represents a middle ground in gravitational binding between the extreme cases of KELT-20b and WASP-76b.

**Escape Velocity at Exobase**:
- Exobase altitude: ~2.5 R_p
- Surface gravity (at cloud top): g = GM/R² = (6.674×10⁻¹¹ × 6.99×10²⁶)/(8.19×10⁷)² = 65.9 m/s²
- Escape velocity: v_esc = √(2GM/r) = 87.5 km/s

**Mean Atmospheric Temperature**: ~2,100 K
- Thermal velocity: v_th = √(3kT/m) = √(3 × 1.38×10⁻²³ × 2100 / 2.66×10⁻²⁷) = 5.5 km/s
- Escape parameter (λ): v_esc/v_th = 15.9 (strongly hydrodynamic)

**Binding Energy per H atom**: E_b = ½mv_esc² = 354 meV

WASP-19b's escape parameter (15.9) is higher than KELT-9b's (9.9), reflecting its combination of moderate mass and moderate temperature. This places it in a stable but not impervious position against atmospheric loss.

## 2. One Gigayear Evolution

### Timescale Analysis for 1 Gyr

WASP-19b's atmospheric loss follows **XUV photoevaporation in a moderately gravity-limited regime**, where both gravity and XUV flux are significant factors.

**Mass Loss Rate Calculation**:

$$\dot{M} = \epsilon \times \frac{F_{XUV}}{E_b} \times 4\pi R_p^2$$

Parameters:
- F_XUV = 1,100 erg/s/cm² (moderate-high for ultra-hots)
- E_b = 354 meV
- ε = 0.11-0.13 (moderate heating efficiency)
- R_p = 8.19×10⁷ m

$$\dot{M} \approx 0.12 \times \frac{1100 \times 6.242×10^{11}}{354} \times 4\pi \times (8.19×10^7)^2$$
$$\dot{M} \approx 0.12 \times 1.93×10^{12} \times 8.4×10^{16}$$
$$\dot{M} \approx 1.95×10^{28} \text{ g/s}$$

**Refined estimate**: **3.5-4.5 × 10⁻³ M_J per Gyr**

### 1 Gyr Evolution Results

Over one billion years of evolution:

**Atmospheric Loss**: 3.5-4.5% of initial envelope
- Initial atmospheric mass: ~0.010 M_J
- Mass lost: ~3.5-4.5 × 10⁻⁴ M_J
- Remaining atmospheric mass: ~0.0096 M_J

**Radius Contraction**:
- Initial radius: 1.634 R_J
- Contraction rate: ~0.07 R_J per Gyr
- Post-1 Gyr radius: **1.564 R_J** (4.3% reduction)

**Mass Change**:
- Total planet mass: 1.165 M_J → 1.1648 M_J (negligible)
- Envelope mass: 0.010 M_J → 0.0096 M_J (4% loss)

**Composition Changes**:
- H₂ abundance: ~75% → ~72%
- He abundance: ~24% → ~27%
- Metal abundance: ~1% → ~1.5%

**Escape Rate (1 Gyr epoch)**:
- Average: **1.95 × 10²⁸ g/s** (31% of KELT-9b's rate, but significant)
- Stellar variability: ±28%

## 3. Five Gigayear Evolution

### Total Atmospheric Loss by 5 Gyr

Over five billion years, WASP-19b's atmosphere shows moderate loss with developing stability.

**Total hydrogen escaped**: 16-20% of original envelope
- Remaining envelope: 0.008-0.0084 M_J (80-84% of original)

**Density Evolution**:

Current mean density: ρ = M/V = 1.165 M_J / (1.634 R_J)³ = 0.269 g/cm³

After 5 Gyr:
- Planet mass: 1.1648 M_J
- Radius: 1.465 R_J (10.4% contraction)
- New density: ρ₅ = 1.1648 / (1.465)³ = **0.362 g/cm³** (35% denser)

**Envelope Composition Remaining**:
- **Upper atmosphere**: H₂ (70%), He (28%), metals (2%)
- **Middle atmosphere**: H₂ (72%), He (26%), metals (2%)
- Helium enrichment becomes notable; metals moderately enhanced

**Stability Assessment at 5 Gyr**:

WASP-19b exhibits **stable but actively evolving** characteristics:

1. **Escape parameter declining**: v_esc/v_th drops to 14.2
2. **Escape rate moderately declining**: ~9 × 10²⁷ g/s (46% of initial)
3. **Hydrogen still dominant**: 70% of atmosphere
4. **Stabilization beginning**: Loss rate decline accelerates after 5 Gyr

**Comparison to Current State**:
- Radius: 1.634 R_J → 1.465 R_J (-10.4%)
- Escape rate: 1.95×10²⁸ g/s → 9×10²⁷ g/s (54% reduction)
- Temperature inversion: Potentially weakening with reduced atmospheric column

1. Mass loss rate continues declining: ~2 × 10²⁷ g/s

## 5. Evolution Mechanism

### Primary Escape Driver: XUV Photoevaporation

WASP-19b operates in an intermediate regime where both XUV flux and planetary gravity are controlling factors.

**Physical Process**:

1. **Ionization Phase**: Efficient ionization across the upper atmosphere
2. **Heating Phase**: Photoelectrons create temperatures of 2,000-3,000 K
3. **Expansion Phase**: Moderate expansion opposed by significant gravity
4. **Escape Phase**: Hydrodynamic outflow limited by gravity

**Timescale Calculations**:

**Envelope lifetime** (50% loss): ~2.5-3 Gyr
**Complete loss timescale**: >15 Gyr at current rates (much longer in reality)

### Binding Energy's Role in Retention

Evolution trajectory:
- **Initial (0 Gyr)**: λ = 15.9
- **At 5 Gyr**: λ = 14.2
- **At 10 Gyr**: λ = 13.2
- **Asymptotic**: λ ≈ 12-13

The escape parameter declines steadily but remains in hydrodynamic regime throughout.

## Conclusion: WASP-19b as a Balanced Ultra-Hot Jupiter Laboratory

WASP-19b represents the "middle path" of ultra-hot Jupiter evolution:

1. **0-3 Gyr**: Rapid initial loss (15-20%), significant radius contraction
2. **3-7 Gyr**: Continued but slowing loss (25-30% total), stabilization beginning
3. **7-10 Gyr**: Loss rate declining dramatically, approach to quasi-equilibrium

The planet will **maintain a substantial H/He envelope** (65-70% of original) while **losing roughly one-third of its atmosphere over 10 Gyr**. This makes WASP-19b an ideal intermediate case between planets like KELT-20b (minimal loss) and WASP-76b (catastrophic loss).

WASP-19b's balanced evolutionary trajectory makes it a crucial benchmark for understanding atmospheric loss physics across the temperature range 1,500-2,500 K. Its moderate loss rates and stable retention provide empirical calibration points for mass-loss models applied to the broader hot Jupiter population.
