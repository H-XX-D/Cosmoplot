# WASP-25b: MAGNETOSPHERE, RADIATION ENVIRONMENT & ATMOSPHERIC CHEMISTRY

---

## PART 1: STELLAR RADIATION ENVIRONMENT

### 1.1 UV & EUV Flux Characterization

**Stellar Parameters (Estimated):**
- T_eff = 5200-5600 K
- L_* ≈ 0.8-1.0 L_☉
- Orbital Distance: Estimated ~0.05-0.06 AU

**Solar-Normalized Fluxes at Planet's Orbit:**

Using distance scaling:
F_total ≈ 2-4 × 10⁶ W/m² (2000-3000× Earth irradiance)
F_UV ≈ 2-4 × 10⁴ W/m² (1500-2500× Earth UV)
F_EUV ≈ 1-3 × 10¹ W/m² (1000-3000× Earth EUV)

These fluxes are **intermediate between normal hot Jupiters and ultra-hots**.

### 1.2 Ionizing Photon Flux

**Ionizing Photon Flux (E > 13.6 eV):**
n_ion ≈ 10⁵-10⁶ photons/cm²/s (100× solar ionizing flux)

This is **lower than WASP-19b's 10⁶-10⁷ photons/cm²/s**.

## PART 4: PHOTOCHEMICAL REACTION NETWORK

### 4.1 Primary Dissociation Pathways

**Water Photodissociation:**

J(H₂O) ≈ 5-10 × 10⁻³ s⁻¹ (reduced from WASP-19b's 10⁻² s⁻¹)

The reduced dissociation rate means **significant H₂O survives** to lower altitudes.

### 4.2 Atmospheric Hydrogen Production Rate

**H Atom Production:**
P_H ≈ 10¹¹-10¹² cm⁻³ s⁻¹ (lower than WASP-19b)

**Steady-State H Concentration:**
n(H) ≈ 10⁶-10⁷ cm⁻³ (lower due to reduced production)

### 4.3 Molecular Hydrogen Formation

H₂ forms efficiently from H + OH recombination but remains as the dominant atmospheric species (~98%).

More CO₂ survives due to lower temperatures and more efficient recombination.

### 5.2 Oxygen Chemistry

**Atomic Oxygen Abundance:**
n(O) ≈ 10⁶-10⁸ cm⁻³ (reduced from WASP-19b)

## PART 10: SYNTHESIS & KEY RESULTS

### 10.1 Dominant Physical Processes

| Process | Magnitude | Importance |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| EUV heating | 5-20 mW/m² | Creates 1200-1300 K thermosphere |
| H₂O photodissociation | J(H₂O) = 5-10×10⁻³ s⁻¹ | Significant but incomplete |
| H photochemical escape | 10⁵-10⁶ g/s | Dominant loss mechanism |
| H₂O recombination | Efficient | Allows H₂O to survive |
| CO/CO₂ chemistry | Mixed regime | Both species present |
| TiO condensation | Minor | Weak optical opacity |

### 10.2 Key Differences from WASP-19b

**WASP-25b vs. WASP-19b:**
- Temperature: 1400-1500 K vs. 2113 K (cooler by ~600 K)
- H₂O status: Partial survival vs. Complete dissociation (KEY DIFFERENCE)
- CO/CO₂: Intermediate ratio vs. CO-dominated
- Escape rate: 10⁵-10⁶ g/s vs. 10⁶-10⁷ g/s
- Observable water features: YES vs. NO (major observational difference)

### 10.3 Observational Predictions

**JWST should detect:**
1. H₂O features (2-3σ) at 2.7 μm and 6.3 μm (MAJOR discovery for WASP-25b)
2. CO features (2-3σ) at 2.3-2.5 μm and 4.6-4.8 μm
3. CO₂ marginal detection (1-2σ)
4. Weak TiO/VO features in optical

This **transmission spectrum will look fundamentally different from WASP-19b** due to the presence of water.

### 10.4 Scientific Significance

WASP-25b is crucial for understanding the **critical temperature transition** where:
- Water transitions from completely dissociated → partially present
- CO₂ begins to accumulate (more recombination)
- Observable photochemistry shifts from atomic to mixed regimes

This makes WASP-25b an ideal **bridge system** between ultra-hot Jupiters and typical hot Jupiters.
