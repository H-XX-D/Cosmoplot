# WASP-25b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

WASP-25b is a hot Jupiter (Teq ≈ 1513 K) with mass 0.58 M_J and radius 1.27 R_J orbiting an F-type star every 3.77 days. Moderately dense for a hot Jupiter, WASP-25b represents a classic case of carbon-oxygen chemistry at intermediate hot Jupiter temperatures.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 96-98%
- **Water vapor**: ~1-2%
- **Methane**: ~0.2-0.4%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.3-0.5%
- **Atomic H**: 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴

### Bulk Density: 0.36 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 18-22 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_J) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1 | 1513 |
| 0.8 | 12 | 1300 |
| 0.6 | 120 | 1100 |
| 0.4 | 1,200 | 900 |
| 0.2 | 12,000 | 750 |
| 0.0 | 120,000 | 650 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~1513 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar**: 1513-1600 K
- **Terminator**: 1250-1480 K
- **Limb**: 1050-1250 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.76-0.84 |
| He | 0.14-0.20 |
| H₂O | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |
| CO | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |
| H (atomic) | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ |

### Clouds and Hazes

- **Silicate clouds**: Mg₂SiO₄ at 1200-1300 K
- **Sulfur aerosols**: Possible
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 0.4-1.0

## Nightside Chemistry (~1000 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar**: 1000-1100 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 450-550 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance | Ratio |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| H₂O | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.5-1.0 |
| CH₄ | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 1.0-3.0 |
| CO | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ | 0.5-1.0 |
| NH₃ | 10⁻⁵ - 10⁻⁴ | 1.0-2.0 |

### Condensates

- **Silicate clouds**: Extended
- **Water clouds**: Forming in cool regions
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 1.0-1.5

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Emission Spectrum

- **3.2 μm**: H₂O feature
- **4.7 μm**: CO (40-100 ppm)
- **8 μm**: CH₄ (50-100 ppm)

### Transmission Spectrum

- **Water vapor**: 120-300 ppm (dominant feature)
- **Methane**: 40-100 ppm
- **CO**: 20-60 ppm

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 3.2 μm | 100-180 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 80-150 | CO + H₂O |
| 8 μm | 90-160 | CH₄ |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~3-5σ
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~2-3σ
3. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ

---

## Summary

WASP-25b is a classic hot Jupiter with balanced H₂O/CO/CH₄ chemistry. Moderate dayside temperature (1513 K) and 450-550 K day-night gradient create ideal conditions for molecular detection. Water vapor dominates transmission features, while CO and methane are measurable. This world represents the canonical hot Jupiter target for carbon-oxygen chemistry studies.
