## 4. Photochemistry (Intermediate M-dwarf)

### Temperature + Star Classification

At 635 K with strong M-dwarf UV and MODERATE intensity:

**Photochemical Regime**: MODERATELY ACTIVE
- Temperature enables photochemical reactions
- Stellar intensity: Sufficient for species dissociation
- Result: Balanced between frozen and runaway photochemistry

### Reaction Network

**Primary Water Channel:**
```
H₂O + hν(EUV) → H + OH [Moderate rate]
OH + H₂ → H₂O + H [Efficient recycling]
Result: H₂O maintained at high abundance (1-3%)
```

**Secondary Methane Channel:**
```
CH₄ + OH → CH₃ + H₂O [Significant loss]
CH₃ + HO₂ → CH₄ + O₂ [Regeneration at moderate rate]
CH₃ + CH₃ → C₂H₆ [Slow polymerization]
Result: CH₄ balance at 0.2-0.5%
```

**Tertiary Sulfur Channel:**
```
H₂S + OH → SH + H₂O [Slow at intermediate flux]
SO₂ production: Possible but limited
Result: SO₂ at 0.01-0.1%
```

### Photochemical Timescales

| Species | Lifetime |
|---------|----------|
| OH | 0.1 seconds |
| H | 1 second |
| CH₃ | 10 seconds |
| CH₄ | 100-200 years |
| CO | 30-50 years |

**Interpretation**: Intermediate between slow (cool) and fast (hot) photochemistry
