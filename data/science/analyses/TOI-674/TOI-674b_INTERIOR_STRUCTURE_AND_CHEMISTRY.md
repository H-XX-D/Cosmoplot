# TOI-674b: Interior Structure and Day-Night Chemistry

## Executive Summary

TOI-674b is a cool sub-Neptune (Teq ≈ 580 K) with mass 5 M_E and radius 2.3 R_E orbiting a K-star every 1.82 days. This temperate sub-Neptune shows strong atmospheric features with prominent water, methane, and ammonia at cooler equilibrium temperature enabling ice cloud formation.

---

## Interior Composition and Structure

### Atmospheric Composition

- **Hydrogen and Helium**: 70-80% (smaller core relative to envelope)
- **Water vapor**: ~5-15%
- **Methane**: ~2-5%
- **Ammonia**: ~1-3%
- **Carbon monoxide**: ~0.1-0.3%
- **Nitrogen (N₂)**: ~5-10%

### Bulk Density: ~0.6 g/cm³

### Core Mass: 15-20 M_⊕

### Pressure-Temperature Profile

| Depth (R_E) | Pressure (bar) | Temperature (K) |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 2.3 | 1 | 580 |
| 2.0 | 5 | 550 |
| 1.7 | 30 | 500 |
| 1.4 | 150 | 450 |
| 1.1 | 750 | 400 |
| 0.8 | 3,750 | 350 |

## Dayside Chemistry (~580 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Sub-stellar**: 580-620 K
- **Terminator**: 520-580 K

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance |
|---------|-----------|
| H₂ | 0.70-0.80 |
| He | 0.08-0.15 |
| N₂ | 0.05-0.10 |
| H₂O | 10⁻² - 10⁻¹ |
| CH₄ | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² |
| NH₃ | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² |
| CO | 10⁻⁴ - 10⁻³ |

### Clouds and Hazes

- **Water clouds**: Dominant cloud layer
- **Ammonia clouds**: Extensive
- **Ice particles**: Forming in cooler regions
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 2.0-4.0 (very cloudy)

## Nightside Chemistry (~450 K)

### Temperature Profile

- **Anti-stellar**: 450-500 K
- **Day-night ΔT**: 80-150 K (small)

### Dominant Molecules

| Species | Abundance | Ratio |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| H₂O | 10⁻² - 10⁻¹ | 1.0 |
| CH₄ | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² | 1.0-1.2 |
| NH₃ | 10⁻³ - 10⁻² | 1.0-1.2 |
| N₂ | 0.05-0.10 | 1.0 |

### Condensates

- **Water ice**: Forming extensively
- **Ammonia ice**: Possible in coldest regions
- **Methane ice**: At poles/nightside
- **Optical depth**: τ ~ 3.0-5.0 (ice clouds)

## Observable Consequences for JWST

### Predicted Transmission Spectrum

- **Rayleigh haze**: 150-300 ppm (from ice particles)
- **Water vapor**: 400-800 ppm (very strong, ice cloud tops)
- **Methane**: 200-400 ppm (strong)
- **Ammonia**: 150-300 ppm (strong)

### Secondary Eclipse Depths

| Wavelength | Depth (ppm) | Molecule |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 1 μm | 200-400 | Continuum + ice clouds |
| 3.2 μm | 300-600 | H₂O |
| 4.7 μm | 250-500 | CO + H₂O |
| 8 μm | 300-600 | CH₄ |
| 10 μm | 200-400 | NH₃ + ice |

### Detectable Molecules

1. **H₂O**: SNR ~8-15σ (very strong ice cloud signature)
2. **CH₄**: SNR ~4-8σ (strong)
3. **NH₃**: SNR ~4-8σ (strong)
4. **CO**: SNR ~1-3σ (weak)

---

## Summary

TOI-674b is a cool sub-Neptune where ice cloud formation becomes dominant atmospheric feature. Extensive water/ammonia ice clouds create enormous transmission features (400-800 ppm for water). Methane and ammonia abundances significantly higher at cool temperatures. This world represents the first opportunity to study atmospheric ice formation and condensate physics at exoplanet scale with JWST. Multi-molecular detection easily achievable across multiple wavelengths.
